--- layout: null section-type: about title: About --- ## About I am a physics Ph.D. candidate at The University of Chicago. I graduated summa cum laude from Kalamazoo College, MI, where I pursued physics and chemistry. I also studied engineering physics at Yokohama National University (YNU) in Japan. My research interests span the field of soft condensed matter physics. Soft materials consist of building block that is a lot larger than atoms but a lot smaller than their overall system size. I am interested in mechanisms how this mesoscopic length/time scale leads to collective behavior of matter. Currently, my research focuses on turbulence. I was born in Kanagawa, Japan, have lived in Frankfurt, Germany for approximately four years during my teenage years. I participated the 36th Harry Messel International Science School at The University of Sydney, Australia, as a national representative. This experience encouraged me to pursue a career in science and technology. At YNU, I was involved in Student Formula Japan as a mechanic on the chassis team. At Kalamazoo College, I conducted research in theoretical condensed matter physics, high energy physics, and computational neuroscience.
